## Design Context

### Users
Parking lot operators and drivers at commercial/institutional facilities. Quick in-and-out interactions primarily on mobile devices, often in bright outdoor conditions or transitioning between daylight and dim parking garages. The job: get in, park, find the spot, get out — with zero friction.

### Brand Personality
**Functional, Confident, Fast**
The interface should feel like infrastructure you trust — a well-made transit system. Not decorative, not playful, not luxury. Authoritative clarity.

### Aesthetic Direction
**Swiss Transit / Industrial Wayfinding**
Inspired by Tokyo metro maps, Swiss railway signage, airport wayfinding systems. High-contrast, generous whitespace, bold numbering, systematic color-coded status. Light mode — drivers are typically in daylight or transitioning to/from it.

- **Display font**: Archivo Black — blocky, industrial, wayfinding-grade condensed sans
- **Body font**: Manrope — geometric, warm, excellent small-size readability
- **Primary color**: Deep teal — institutional confidence
- **Accent color**: Signal orange — urgency, CTAs
- **Theme**: Light mode (outdoor daylight context)

**Anti-references**: Dark sci-fi control panels, Art Deco gold-and-black, glassmorphism dashboards, gradient text, glow effects.

### Design Principles
1. **Clarity over style** — Every element must communicate, not decorate
2. **Color encodes status** — Green = available, Red = occupied, Blue = selected, Orange = action needed
3. **Wayfinding-grade hierarchy** — A driver glancing at their phone should understand the layout instantly
4. **Respect the context** — Light mode for daylight, large touch targets for one-handed use, fast load for mobile
5. **No jargon** — Plain language a first-time visitor understands immediately
